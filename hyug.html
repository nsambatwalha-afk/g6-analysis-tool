To help with your Water Resources Engineering exercises, here are the solutions for Questions 2 (statistical analysis of Ugandan gauging station data) and 5 (exponential distribution fitting for rainfall). --- ## Question 2: Statistical Analysis of Peak Discharge [cite_start]The dataset consists of 16 years of annual peak discharge (cumecs)[cite: 2]. ### 1. Data Organization First, we sort the 16 values in ascending order to facilitate calculation: [cite_start]1557, 1625, 1913, 2157, 2405, 2602, 2662, 2934, 3120, 3655, 3670, 4005, 4621, 4630, 6216, 6403[cite: 2]. ### 2. Measures of Central Tendency * **Mean ($\bar{x}$):** Sum of all values divided by $n=16$. $$\bar{x} = \frac{54175}{16} = 3385.94 \text{ cumecs}$$ * **Median:** The average of the 8th and 9th values in the sorted list. $$\text{Median} = \frac{2934 + 3120}{2} = 3027 \text{ cumecs}$$ * **Mode:** There are no repeating values in this dataset, so it is **amodal**. ### 3. Measures of Spread (Dispersion) * **Range:** $6403 - 1557 = 4846 \text{ cumecs}$. * **Standard Deviation ($s$):** Using the sample formula $s = \sqrt{\frac{\sum (x_i - \bar{x})^2}{n-1}}$. $$s \approx 1452.34 \text{ cumecs}$$ * **Coefficient of Variation ($C_v$):** $$C_v = \frac{s}{\bar{x}} = \frac{1452.34}{3385.94} \approx 0.429 \text{ (or 42.9\%)}$$ ### 4. Measures of Asymmetry and Flatness * **Skewness ($C_s$):** This measures the lack of symmetry. Using the sample skewness formula: $$C_s \approx 0.81$$ [cite_start]**Comment:** Since $C_s > 0$, the data is **positively skewed** (skewed to the right), meaning there are a few years with exceptionally high flood peaks that pull the mean higher than the median[cite: 3, 4]. * **Kurtosis ($C_k$):** This measures the "peakedness" or flatness. $$C_k \approx -0.12$$ [cite_start]**Comment:** The value is close to 0 (near-normal), but slightly negative, indicating a **platykurtic** distribution (slightly flatter than a normal distribution)[cite: 3, 4]. --- ## Question 5: Exponential Distribution of Rainfall [cite_start]You are given grouped rainfall data for 1 year (365 days)[cite: 5, 6]. ### 1. Relative Frequency Histogram First, we calculate the total number of rainy days recorded in the table: [cite_start]$90+49+34+17+13+5+3+2+1+1 = 215 \text{ days}$[cite: 7]. *(Note: The remaining 150 days of the year are assumed to have 0 mm rainfall).* [cite_start]To plot the relative frequency, divide the number of days in each class by 215. For example, the 0–10 mm class has a relative frequency of $90/215 \approx 0.418$[cite: 7]. ### 2. Fitting the Exponential Distribution [cite_start]The probability density function for an exponential distribution is $f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x}$[cite: 8]. The parameter $\lambda$ is $1/\bar{x}$. * **Step A: Find the Mean ($\bar{x}$)** Using midpoints ($m$) of classes: $\bar{x} = \frac{\sum (f \cdot m)}{\sum f} = \frac{(90 \cdot 5) + (49 \cdot 15) + (34 \cdot 25) + \dots + (1 \cdot 95)}{215}$ $\bar{x} \approx \frac{4655}{215} \approx 21.65 \text{ mm}$ * **Step B: Determine $\lambda$** $$\lambda = \frac{1}{21.65} \approx 0.0462 \text{ mm}^{-1}$$ ### 3. Probability Estimate ($P(X > 45 \text{ mm})$) [cite_start]For an exponential distribution, the exceedance probability is $P(X > x) = e^{-\lambda x}$[cite: 8]. $$P(X > 45) = e^{-(0.0462 \cdot 45)}$$ $$P(X > 45) = e^{-2.079} \approx 0.125$$ [cite_start]**Result:** There is approximately a **12.5% chance** that a randomly selected rainy day will have rainfall exceeding 45 mm[cite: 8].
